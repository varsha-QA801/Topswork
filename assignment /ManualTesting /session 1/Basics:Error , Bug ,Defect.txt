2.
Write a short explanation (2-3 sentences each) for the following: What is the difference between an error, a bug, and a defect? Give one real-world example for each from any app you use daily (e.g., Instagram, Paytm, IRCTC).

Answer :
(a) ERROR
An error is a mistake made by a person while writing code or designing software.
It happens because of wrong logic, typing mistakes, or misunderstanding the requirement.
     Example: A developer writes the wrong formula for calculating discounts in Paytm.
     
(b) Bug
A bug is a problem found in the software while testing or using the app.
It makes the software behave differently than expected.
     Example: Instagram crashes when opening the Reels section.
     
(c) Defect
A defect is a confirmed issue where the software does not meet the expected requirement.
It is reported after testing and needs to be fixed by the developer.
    Example: On IRCTC, the payment is successful but the ticket is not generated.

Think of a recent time when an app you use (like WhatsApp, BookMyShow, or PhonePe) didn't work as expected. Identify the possible cause of the defect: was it a human error, a technical limitation, or a process gap? Explain your reasoning for each case.<br><br><em><strong>Hint:</strong> Consider examples like payment failures, app crashes, or incorrect notifications.</em>

Answer:
A recent problem I faced was in PhonePe. I made a payment, and the money was deducted from my bank account, but the payment showed Pending for some time.

  (a) Human error: This was probably not caused by a human mistake because I entered the correct details.
  (b) Technical limitation: It may have happened because of a slow internet connection or a temporary server issue between the bank and the app.
  (c) Process gap: The app may not have updated the payment status immediately, showing that the process for syncing payment information needs improvement.
  
4.
Imagine you are testing a new food delivery app (like Swiggy). List 3 types of risks that could affect the quality of the app before launch (for example: security risk, usability risk, performance risk). For each risk, briefly explain how it could impact users.

Answer:
(a) Security Risk:
   If the app does not protect user data or payment details, hackers may steal personal information. This can reduce user trust in the app.

(b) Usability Risk:
   If the app is difficult to use or the ordering process is confusing, users may not be able to place orders easily. This can make users leave the app.

(c) Performance Risk:
   If the app is slow, crashes, or takes too much time to load, users may face delays while ordering food. This can lead to a poor user experience and fewer customers.
   
5.
You are given a mini project: a Movie Ticket Booking webpage. Classify the following as either a defect or a risk: (a) The 'Book Now' button does not respond on mobile, (b) There is no logout option, (c) The app sometimes loads slowly during IPL match days. Justify your answer for each.

Answer:
(a) 'Book Now' button does not respond on mobile – Defect
     This is a defect because the button is not working as expected. Users cannot book movie tickets on mobile.

(b) There is no logout option – Defect
    This is a defect because a logout option is an important feature. Users cannot safely sign out of their accounts.

(c) The app sometimes loads slowly during IPL match days – Risk
    This is a risk because the app may become slow when many users use it at the same time. This can affect the user experience.
